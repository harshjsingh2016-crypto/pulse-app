Pulse — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 10 July 2026

Pulse ("we", "the app") is a personal life-management app for tracking tasks, meals, workouts, spending, and habits through conversation. This policy explains what we collect, how it is used, and the third parties that process it. By using Pulse you agree to this policy.

Information we collect

  • Account: your email address, used to sign you in with a one-time code.
  • Content you create: tasks, subtasks, notes, domains, meals and their macros, workouts, spending entries and budgets, recurring habits, and your chat messages.
  • Optional health profile: if you choose to provide it, your age, weight, height, and free-text health notes (which may include conditions such as diabetes). This is used only to personalize nutrition and fitness suggestions in chat. You can view, edit, or clear it at any time.
  • Voice input: if you use the microphone, the audio clip is sent for transcription and the resulting text is placed in your message box. We do not store the audio.
  • Usage metadata: counts of AI messages and token/cost totals, used for rate limits and cost monitoring.

How we use your information

  • To provide the app's features and store your data so it's available across your sessions.
  • To generate AI responses, macro estimates, and personalized suggestions based on your messages, your relevant app data, and (where provided) your health profile.
  • To send you sign-in codes and to enforce usage limits.

We do not sell your data, and we do not use it for advertising.

Third-party processors

We rely on the following service providers, who process your data only to provide these features:

  • Google Firebase — authentication, database, hosting, and serverless functions (stores your account and content).
  • Anthropic (Claude) and OpenAI — power the chat assistant, meal-macro estimates, and voice transcription. When you send a chat message or use voice input, your message, relevant app-data context, and (if set) your health profile are sent to the selected provider to generate a response. Under these providers' API terms, this content is not used to train their models.
  • Resend — sends your sign-in code emails.

Delete your Pulse account

This section explains how to delete your account for Pulse (developed by Harsh Singh), what gets deleted, what (if anything) is retained, and for how long.

How to request deletion

  1. In the app (immediate): open Pulse → tap the ⋯ menu at the top of any tab → Account → Delete account → confirm. Your account and data are deleted right away.
  2. Without the app (if you've uninstalled it): email harshjsingh2016@gmail.com from the email address on your account with the subject "Delete my Pulse account." We verify the request and delete your account within 7 days, then confirm by email.

What gets deleted

Deleting your account permanently and irreversibly removes, immediately (or within 7 days for email requests):

  • Your account and sign-in credentials (email, authentication record)
  • All tasks, subtasks, domains, and recurring habits
  • All meal, workout, and spending entries and budget categories
  • Your full chat history with the assistant
  • Your health profile (age, weight, height, health notes), if you provided one
  • Your usage and rate-limit records

What is retained, and for how long

  • Nothing is retained in Pulse's own systems. Account deletion is a full deletion of your user record and every subcollection in our database — we keep no backup copy after deletion.
  • AI providers (Anthropic, OpenAI): messages and context sent to generate chat replies or macro estimates may be retained briefly by these providers (typically up to 30 days) under their own API data-retention policies for abuse and safety monitoring, independent of when you delete your Pulse account. This content is not used to train their models per their API terms.
  • Crash diagnostics (Sentry): we do not attach your account or email to crash reports, so they are anonymous and not affected by account deletion; Sentry retains them per its own default retention period.
  • Sign-in emails (Resend): may appear briefly in Resend's own email-delivery logs, independent of your Pulse account.

Sign-in codes themselves always expire within 10 minutes regardless of account status.

Security

Data is transmitted over encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS) and stored on Google Firebase infrastructure with per-user access controls, so only your authenticated account can access your data.

Children

Pulse is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from them.
